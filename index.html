How To Create A Song

Songwriting
Demo Tapes
Shaping Air Molecules

Step 1:

Once a melody, chords or a beat hits you, record it on any accessible device. If its a melody, work on the chords around it. If its chords, hum a melody over it. If its a rhythm, hum a bass line to it. Write lyrics if it helps, or avoid it if it gets in the way.
Write at least 1 contrasting section. Shape the 2 sections into a beginning, middle and end.

Arranging
Writing the parts
Shaping Dots On Paper

Step 2:

Select a style by playing with rhythms and tempo. Find a key that works best for all instruments in the song. Feel the tempo that supports the melody and lyrics. Make each section unique by adding or subtracting elements.

Rehearsing
Working the parts
Title description, Dec 7, 2017

Step 3:

Split rehearsal into groups

  1. Drum & Bass
  2. Rhythm section
  3. Harmony section
  4. Background harmony
  5. Everyone together

Working with individual sections will weave out small errors. Rehearse until the song is memorized and can be played effortlessly.
Practice is for the individual, rehearsal is for the group.

Performing
Playing the parts
It's Alive!

Step 4:

Your practice has paid off and its time to lay down tracks. Recording is a mental game. The red flashing dot can throw us off but it's important to clear the mind, and allow muscle memory to take over. Records have feel, and that feeling comes from the intent of the player.
This is where the song comes to life. We can only work on what has been recorded.

Producing
Mixing the parts
Solidify The Process

Step 5:

Tracks are recorded and its time to mix. Use EQ's to clear up sounds that occupy similar frequency; apply compression as needed (based on style); add effects (reverb,delay,chorus,flanger) to blend something in, or make it stand out; automate any of these functions to contrast each section.
Find 3 to 5 songs you really like and use them to A/B your mix as you go along.
